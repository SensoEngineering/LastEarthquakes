1. INTRODUCTION

This post-event report is prepared based on the Eurasia Tunnel Operations Guidelines for Earthquake Situations. According to that, a short summary report needs to be prepared for earthquakes that satisfy one of the conditions below:

  1. Earthquakes of Mw>=6.0 within the radius of 300 to 400km from the tunnel mid-point or,
  2. Earthquakes of Mw>=5.0 within the radius of 200 to 300km from the tunnel mid-point or,
  3. Earthquakes of Mw>=4.5 within the radius of 100 to 200km from the tunnel mid-point or,
  4. Earthquakes of Mw>=4.0 within the radius of 100km from the tunnel mid-point,

where M is the moment magnitude of the earthquake (Mw or ML, whichever is reported by Kandilli Observatory) and distance is the epicentral distance from the source to the mid-point of the tunnel.

The summary report includes:

  • a short description of the earthquake (date, time, magnitude, location, depth, and distance to the tunnel)
  • maximum PGA values attained between the seismic joints
  • maximum deformations on the seismic joints
  • plot of the accelerations, response spectra and seismic joint displacements.

3. MONITORING SYSTEM AND THE MEASUREMENTS

3.1. Monitoring System

The Eurasia Tunnel SHM system consists of 66 channels (Figure 2). These are:

  • 3 channels x 9 accelerometers along the TBM sections (ACC01-09)
  • 3 channels x 1 accelerometers at VBE (ventilation Europe, VBE01)
  • 3 channels x 2 accelerometers at OBA (Operation & Maintenance Building, OBA01-02)
  • 3 channels x 3 accelerometers at upper deck locations (ACC10-12)
  • 3 channels x 6 displacement sensors at seismic joints (SJ101-103 and SJ201-203)
  • 1 channel x 3 displacement sensors measuring the tunnel diameter at mid-height (Disp1 - 3)

The accelerometers record hourly files with 200Hz sampling rate while the displacement sensors record hourly files with 20Hz sampling rate.

The accelerometer sensors record data in 3 axes simultaneously, named E, N and Z. All accelerometer sensors are placed in a way that their East(E) direction coincides with the tunnel longitudinal axis towards Asia.

3.4. PGA Values at the Surface Buildings

There are 3 accelerometers placed in the surface buildings. The sensors record data in 3 axes simultaneously, named E, N and Z. All accelerometer sensors are placed in a way that their East(E) direction coincides with the tunnel longitudinal axis towards Asia.

In OBA (Operation and Maintenance Building), there are two 3-axis accelerometers. OBA01 is placed in the monitoring room in the 1st floor, while OBA02 is placed on the ground floor at Yemekhane (Canteen). It is thus possible to obtain the response of OBA to the earthquake. In order to do that, a transfer function is created between these two sensors in all 3 directions and given in Figure 4. The period of the peak in the transfer function corresponds to the fundamental period of that structure in the relevant direction. Change in this period may refer to damages. In Figure 4, the zero-measurement period value of the peak responses are also given.

3.5. Deformations on the Seismic Joints

There are 6 laser distance measurements placed on the seismic joints. The displacement limit of these joints is 75mm in longitudinal and 50mm in transverse directions. The sensors record data in 3 axes simultaneously, named E, N and Z. All laser sensors are placed in a way that their East(E) direction coincides with the tunnel longitudinal axis towards Asia.
